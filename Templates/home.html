A famous picture speaks a thousand words, capturing history in a single frame.

The Sanchi Museum, located near the famous Sanchi Stupa in Madhya Pradesh, India, houses a rich collection of artifacts, sculptures, and relics from the Maurya and Gupta periods, offering deep insights into ancient Buddhist art and culture.

The Archaeological Museum, Sanchi

The Archaeological Museum, Sanchi, is an important museum located near the Sanchi Stupa in Madhya Pradesh, India.

The original Ashoka Pillar

These pillars were erected by Emperor Ashoka (268–232 BCE) of the Maurya Dynasty across the Indian subcontinent to spread Buddhist teachings and messages of Dhamma (righteousness).

Sir John Marshall's House

This house was built as a residence for Sir John Marshall while he supervised the excavation and restoration of the Sanchi Buddhist complex in the early 20th century.
